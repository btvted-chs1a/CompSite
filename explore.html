THE COMPONENTS OF A COMPUTER AND THEIR FUNCTIONALITY

System Unit

It is also known as the computer case. This is the section of the computer system that houses every component.

It is typically made to facilitate installing a motherboard, cables, and drives as simply as possible. Some are

so expertly made that maintaining a neat and appealing appearance is simple.

Motherboard

This is the main board that is fastened to the computer chassis with screws. Everything else, including additional

cards, connects directly to the motherboard, thus its name. It has connections for the CPU, RAM, disks, power supply,

and other components. All the physical parts must be integrated to work and communicate together for it to perform its role.

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The CPU can be thought of as the brain of computers. It performs computer processing on each piece of data. It executes every

process in the RAM in order to complete the tasks needed by the computer system. The central processing unit is often installed

in a socket that fastens the CPU to the motherboard using a lever or latches with a hinged plate that has a hole in the middle.

Underneath it, there are several copper pads that the socket contacts must push up against in order to make electrical contact.

Random Access Memory (RAM)

A type of data storage that allows for quick read and write access. RAM is a volatile memory, which means that when power is disconnected

all of the data is lost. It maintains data available for processing by the CPU. The overall speed of a computer system is significantly influenced

by the RAM speed. Additionally, it connects straight into a lengthy slot with contacts on either side. Like a processor, it too has a clock speed.

In order to give performance above and beyond the designed specification, it can also be overclocked.A heat spreader is sold with some RAM modules

It helps the memory ICs stay cooler by dissipating heat from each one of them.

Graphics Card or Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

The data from the motherboard is processed by a graphics card, which is an output device that provides the required data to the computer's screen for display.

wherein you may use HDMI, DisplayPort, DVI, or VGA ports to attach monitors to it. It is also known as a video card or display card.

The main CPU is relieved of all video processing duties by a video card. This significantly improves a computer's performance.

The major component of a computer system that requires cooling is the GPU (Graphics Processing Unit), which is found on a graphics card.

Sound Card

For audio output, this component of the motherboard is typically employed. A computer can connect sound cards in several different ways.

Through a PCI, USB, or PCI Express x 1 slot are all options. The entire audio processing is handled by a sound processing chip on the card,

which is typically a fairly weak processor. It card can provide a variety of connectivity options with different audio equipment.

Hard Disk Drive (HDD)

Many PCs still come with hard drives today. All of your information is stored on a mechanical drive so that it can be retrieved at any moment.

It serves as a boot drive to start your computer's operating system (OS) in addition to serving as a storage device for data. Depending on your demands,

you can install various operating systems. An operating system (OS) is a piece of software that is placed on a computer to enable its operation,

such as Microsoft Windows. The physical fragility of a mechanical drive is its major weakness. One incorrect bump can completely ruin driving.

One or more platters on a mechanical hard drive spin anywhere between 5200 and 10000 RPM (revolutions per minute)

Solid State Drive (SSD)

A different kind of hard drive is an SSD, which has no moving parts. It comprises a bank of flash memory with a respectable quantity of data

storage capacity. A high-performance drive is the SSD. Due to its speed, it cannot be easily broken by being dropped or receiving minor impacts.

Power Supply Unit (PSU)

The computer case has a power supply device mounted inside. It converts the wall outlet power cord's AC mains supply into the proper DC voltages,

which are then sent to all of the computer's internal components. It is crucial to a computer's continued dependability. For power supply, there are many

wattage ratings. The electrical current that can be made accessible to everything that requires it to operate properly increases with increasing wattage. A cooling

fan is typically included with a power supply. This keeps your computer's internal parts from overheating even when the power supply is under heavier stress.

Visual Display Unit (VDU)

An output device, such as a monitor, is used to display the graphical data transmitted by the computer's GPU. In order to

meet the high expectations necessary to remove delays with user input for gaming, monitors also have a quick response time.

Keyboard

One of the ways to interact with a computer is through an input device like a keyboard. When a key is touched

on the keyboard, a tiny amount of data is sent to the computer to let it know which key was pressed.

Mouse

It is an input device that enables more natural interaction with computer systems

by allowing the user to move a pointer that is shown on the monitor.

Computer Speakers

The sound card at the back of the computer can be used to connect computer speakers.

Another approach to connect them is using a monitor with built-in speakers.
